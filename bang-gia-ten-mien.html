RULES FOR REGISTERING A DOMAIN NAME
  • Domain name must not contain over 63 characters, including .com, .net, .org…
  • Domain names only contain alphabetical (a-z) and numeric (0-9) characters and hyphens (-).
  • Spaces and special characters are invalid.
  • A domain name cannot start or end with a hyphen (-).
  • Domain names should be short, memorable, not confusing and not miswritten.
  • Domain names directly relate to the domain holder name and operating field.
  • Domain holders have to proactively pay for maintaining domain names prior to expired date, and be responsible for the loss of domain names due to failure in payment.
RECOMMENDATION

A company’s domain name is directly related to its trademark. If you only focus on building your own trademark on paper and are not concern with your domain name, another individual/organization may take advantage of a domain name related to your trademark. This will result confusion in your customers when they try to access a domain name that has your trademark. To make it worse, if such a domain name is taken by your business rivals, then the results will be loss of accesses and your potential customers. Therefore, Mat Bao recommend you to register the following domain names:

  • International domain name (.com, .net, .org)
  • Viet Nam domain name (.vn, .com.vn, .net.vn)
  • Other recommended new domain (.tel, .asia,…)
